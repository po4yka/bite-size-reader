Privacy Policy

Bite-Size Reader — Effective date: 2026-03-17

1. Introduction

Bite-Size Reader ("the App", "the Service") is a personal, self-hosted tool for AI-powered summarisation of web articles and YouTube videos. This policy describes what data is collected, how it is used, and how it is protected.

2. Data We Collect

  • Telegram user ID — used to authenticate you and associate your content with your account. No phone number or real name is stored.
  • Reading history — URLs you submit, the AI-generated summaries, read/unread status, highlights, and reading time.
  • Preferences — language preference, daily reading goal settings, collection organisation, and digest channel subscriptions.
  • Device session metadata — platform identifier and last-seen timestamp, used to support multi-device sync.

3. Data We Do Not Collect

  • Passwords — the App uses OAuth only (Telegram, Apple, Google Sign-In); no passwords are stored.
  • Payment information — the Service is free and processes no payments.
  • Advertising identifiers or tracking pixels.
  • Contacts, location, camera, or microphone data.

4. How We Use Your Data

  • Authenticate you and maintain your session.
  • Store and serve your article summaries and reading history.
  • Synchronise your data across devices.
  • Deliver scheduled channel digests you have subscribed to.
  • Compute reading statistics shown in the Stats screen.

5. Data Storage

All data is stored in a SQLite database on a self-hosted server controlled solely by the Service operator. No data is stored on third-party cloud databases.

6. Data Sharing

Your data is not shared with any third parties. Article content is sent to the OpenRouter API for AI summarisation; only the article text and URL are transmitted — no personally identifiable information is included in those requests.

7. Data Retention

Your data is retained for as long as your account exists. You may request deletion of your account and all associated data at any time using the "Delete Account" option in the Settings screen.

8. Security

Access tokens are stored in the platform keychain (iOS) or EncryptedSharedPreferences (Android). Server communication uses HTTPS. The server is access-controlled by a Telegram user ID allowlist.

9. Contact

For questions or data requests, please open an issue at github.com/po4yka .
